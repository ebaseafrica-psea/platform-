What Is Sexual Exploitation, Abuse, and Harassment?

Sexual Exploitation: Taking advantage of an individual's vulnerability or power dynamics to engage in sexual activities for personal or third-party gain, without regard for the individual's rights, well-being, or consent.

Sexual Abuse: Any non-consensual sexual activity or behavior, including attempted or threatened acts. Consent cannot be given when there is an imbalance of power.

Sexual Harassment: Unwelcome or unwanted behavior of a sexual nature, including advances, comments, gestures, or actions that create a hostile, intimidating, or offensive environment.

None of These Behaviors Are Ever Acceptable

FAQs

  • Can I report anonymously? – Yes.
  • Will the accused know I reported them? – No.
  • What if the accused is a senior staff or donor? – Everyone is subject to the same policy.
  • Will I face retaliation? – No. Retaliation is prohibited.
  • I witnessed something. Should I still report? – Yes.

What If You're Accused?

  • You will be informed and allowed to respond
  • An internal or external review will be launched
  • Sanctions may include suspension, termination, or legal referral
  • False accusations made maliciously may result in disciplinary action

Survivor Support

  • Immediate safety planning
  • Referrals to health, psychosocial, and legal services
  • Emotional support and counselling
  • Survivor-led approach: your needs and choices come first
